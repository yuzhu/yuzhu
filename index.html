About Me

Welcome to my page. I am a researcher at the University of California, Berkeley, with a deep interest in computer systems. My work focuses on the performance and architecture of distributed systems, operating systems, and databases. I am passionate about building efficient, scalable, and robust systems to tackle large-scale data challenges. My background includes research on data management, information flow tracking, and developing scalable operating systems for many-core architectures.

Technical Interests

My work and interests are centered around creating high-performance computing systems. Key areas include:

  • Distributed Systems: Designing and building scalable systems that can handle big data workloads. I have contributed to major open-source projects like Alluxio and Presto (a distributed SQL query engine).
  • Operating Systems: Researching scalable OS architecture for many-core processors and developing foundational primitives for parallel applications.
  • Database Systems: Exploring challenges in data management, including techniques for automated online schema evolution.
  • System Security: Developing precise information flow tracking systems to prevent sensitive data leaks in applications.
